## About Me

I am a Ph.D. student in the Department of Biological Systems Engineering at Virginia Tech. My research focuses on sustainable technologies for aquaculture and food systems, integrating advanced delivery platforms, biomaterials, and postharvest preservation strategies.

## Current Research

### Delivery Systems for Aquaculture Health

My research develops novel delivery platforms, including PLGA-based microparticles and Pickering emulsions, for the oral administration of probiotics and inactivated pathogens. These systems are designed to enhance immune responses and disease resistance in fish and shrimp aquaculture.

### Passive Radiative Cooling for Food Preservation

I investigate passive radiative cooling materials and films for sustainable temperature regulation and food preservation applications. This work aims to improve food quality and reduce energy consumption during storage and transportation.

## Previous Research

### Microplastic Pollution and Shellfish Health

My master's research examined the physiological and biochemical effects of microplastic exposure on marine bivalves, contributing to a better understanding of environmental stressors in aquatic ecosystems.

### Seafood Preservation and Live Storage

I also worked on preservation technologies for oysters, mussels, and other seafood products, focusing on quality maintenance, shelf-life extension, and live storage strategies.
